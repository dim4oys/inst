## Search setup audit

The previous setup was too technical. Hacker News, GitHub Trending, and Product Hunt are useful for seeing technology shifts, but they bias the system toward developer tools, AI agents, servers, and infrastructure.

For local business ideas, the better search rule is: look close to the transaction. The useful signals are missed calls, manual booking, repeated questions, schedule screenshots, waitlists, no-shows, deposits, open slots, and slow quoting.

## New source mix

| Source type | Why it matters | Current status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Maps and reviews | Shows real customer frustration | primary |
| Telegram/WhatsApp/VK/Instagram groups | Shows manual booking and local demand | primary |
| Booking products | Shows willingness to pay and competitor gaps | primary |
| Owner forums | Shows owner language and workflow pain | secondary |
| Local sports communities | Strong fit for weekly events, substitutions, and payments | primary |
| HN/Product Hunt/GitHub | Technology side-channel only | deprioritized |

## Best first bet

Start with AI-запись на любительские игры, especially volleyball. It has the cleanest MVP:

- Telegram bot or mini-app.
- Game calendar.
- Player level and position.
- Waitlist and substitutions.
- Payment status.
- Reminders.
- Simple organizer dashboard.

This is narrow enough to build quickly and broad enough to expand into pickleball, padel, football, basketball, dance classes, and other recurring group activities.
